World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)

  • Neutral international consortium
  • Founded in 1994 by Tim Berners-Lee (the inventor of the Web)
Main objective: leading the Web to its full potential

Who works at W3C?

  • +70 people as the core Team
  • +450 all-size Members from all sectors
  • Work is developed by +1500 best engineers from those Members, working within +70 working groups

What does W3C do?

Standards

+1000 Technical specifications, guidelines and tools for the Web:

Open standards with perhaps the most transparent patent policy on the Internet.

Members

W3C Advisory Committee (AC)

Each Member organization appoints a representative for the W3C Advisory Committee

  • Consensus-based decisions. In all technical and strategic aspects
  • Any Member may propose new activities or technologies
  • Draw the strategy of W3C. Roadmap
  • Insiders access privilege information. Both strategic and technical
  • AC Representative manages their company's participation at W3C

How does W3C work?

Different types of groups that develop technical work

  • Groups are composed of both Member's personnel and W3C staff
  • Any Member may take part in any working group or activity
  • Groups take decisions based on their member's consensus
  • Work is follow in remote (teleconferences, email, wiki, etc.)
  • Two annual (face-to-face) meetings
  • Early work developed in private and later exposed for public review

Allocation of resources to working groups

Each member may allocate unlimited participants to any group

  • Observer: filtering and reading emails (1h/week)*
  • Pasive: attendance at meetings and following other's work (3h/week)*
  • Active: participation in meetings, editing documents (<8 hours/week)*

* This may vary and there is no commitments. Only shown as reference
